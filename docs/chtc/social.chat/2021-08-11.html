(14:36:30) jcpatton: ML = Mid-level, CAPE = convectively available potential energy... It's a measure of how strong storm updrafts could be once the warm air at/near the ground starts getting lifted. A positive difference between the temperature of lifted air parcels and the temperature of the ambient air (i.e. lifted air warmer than environment) contributes to CAPE. Values > 1000 J/kg are indicative of a severe weather threat
(14:38:20) jcpatton: I’m not exactly sure how mid-level is defined here, I could probably find out
(14:38:41) jcpatton: OH! It’s not “mid-level”, it’s “mixed-layer”
(14:38:47) jcpatton: :facepalm:
(14:39:11) jcpatton: the mixed layer being the turbulent boundary layer in about the lowest 1 km of the atmosphere
(14:39:34) jcpatton: so instead of using surface temperature to compute CAPE, they’re using the mean (potential) temperature of the mixed layer
(14:40:30) jcpatton: (generally speaking, the mean potential temperature of the mixed layer is going to be pretty close to the surface temperature)
