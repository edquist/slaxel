(17:05:10) bbockelm: > Haven't seen the equivalent in the scitokens world at all--seems that only people can request tokens and not machines
Just to clarify a bit in case people don't want to drop to the #scitokens channel: in SciTokens, there's no concept of either people or machines. There's just unique identifiers used for traceability. My subject is 27234843-fedf-42c8-bb81-a1695bbd7c28 in the WLCG issuer, for example.

Now the token _issuer_ can have all sorts of policies about who gets what tokens, who's allowed to renew, whether you can have automated renewals, etc, etc. And that can be part of the rules of the instance of the software - or built in to the token issuer software itself.
