(13:06:01) ajit: @spiperov Since the NN and SNN is the brain of the ~10PB hadoop storage system/clusters (and projected to increase for Run4), I think it's worth investing in 2 physical nodes with just enough power to handle the tasks well for peace of mind (i.e. not to worry about VMs getting beaten, running into swap issues etc).

You can use some of your good old WNs that you plan to retire just because they are out-of-warranty (which otherwise can serve for 4-5 more years with the kind of minimal load those will have as a NN/SNN) OR spend ~$3k (total) on 2 new 1U nodes with 4C/8T CPU, 64G RAM (with room to expand), and 4 disk slots (for a solid raid 10 with 500GB/1TB HDDs) or appropriate number of SSDs (U.2/M.2 etc) to maintain the file system integrity and redundancy in case of issues. The new nodes would/could serve for 10 years with minimal maintenance.
